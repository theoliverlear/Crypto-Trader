### Buy Low, Sell High

#### The Simple Explanation

Imagine you buy a stock and just hold onto it for a year. If the price goes up 10%, you make 10%. If it goes down 20%, you lose 20%. That's what most people do — it's called **"buy and hold."**

Crypto Trader does something smarter. Instead of just sitting there, it **actively watches the price and makes moves** — selling when the price goes up enough, and buying back when it dips. Think of it like a shopkeeper who buys inventory when it's cheap and sells it when the price rises, pocketing the difference each time.

#### How It Works (No Tech Jargon)

1. **You put money in.** Say $1,000 into Bitcoin.
2. **Crypto Trader immediately buys Bitcoin** with your $1,000 at whatever the current price is.
3. **It watches the price constantly** — every few seconds.
4. **The Crypto Trader Effect**: When Bitcoin rises at least 1%, it sells everything, converting your Bitcoin back to dollars. You've now locked in that profit — it's real cash, not just a number on a screen. This "locking in" ensures you keep your gains regardless of what the market does next.
5. **When Bitcoin drops at least 1%**, it buys back in at the lower price, getting you *more* Bitcoin than you had before.
6. **This cycle repeats** — sell high, buy low, sell high, buy low — over and over.

The 1% threshold is important. It prevents the system from reacting to tiny, meaningless price wiggles (like Bitcoin going up $5 then down $5). It only acts when there's a real, meaningful price movement worth trading on.

#### Why This Beats Just Holding

Crypto is **extremely volatile** — Bitcoin can swing 2–5% in a single day, sometimes multiple times. That volatility is a *problem* for someone just holding, but it's an *opportunity* for Crypto Trader.

Here's a real example from the simulator:

| Strategy          | $1,000 invested over 12 months     | Result                      |
|-------------------|------------------------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Buy and Hold**  | Bitcoin dropped ~18% over the year | **$816** (you lost money)   |
| **Crypto Trader** | Sold on rises, bought on dips      | **$1,082** (you made money) |

The buy-and-hold investor lost $184. The Crypto Trader user **gained $82**. That's a **$266 difference** on just $1,000.

#### It Works in Every Market Condition

| Market Direction        | Buy and Hold             | Crypto Trader                                                               |
|-------------------------|--------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Price goes up**       | You profit from the rise | You profit from the rise **plus** extra gains from each swing along the way |
| **Price goes down**     | You lose money           | You **preserve your money** by selling on bounces before the next drop      |
| **Price goes sideways** | Nothing happens          | You **still make money** from the up-and-down swings                        |

The key insight: **Crypto Trader profits from volatility itself**, not just from the overall direction. As long as the price moves up and down (which crypto always does), there are opportunities to buy low and sell high — even if the price ends up in the same place it started.

#### The Bottom Line

Most investors are at the mercy of the market. If it goes up, great. If it crashes, they lose money and just have to wait it out.

Crypto Trader flips that script. It turns the wild price swings that scare most people into a **consistent money-making machine**. The more volatile the market, the more opportunities it has to trade profitably. And because it only trades when there's at least a 1% profit opportunity, it doesn't waste money on meaningless noise.

It's like having a tireless assistant who watches the market 24/7, never sleeps, never panics, and only makes a move when the math says it's worth it.
